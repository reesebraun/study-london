Prague

Your Fairytale Escape

Best for: Budget travelers, architecture lovers, nightlife • 2–3 days

The Vibe

Prague looks like someone built a fairy tale and forgot to tell the 21st century about it. Every corner is photogenic. Gothic spires, old bridges, weird statues, underground taverns—it's genuinely beautiful.

The downside: it's absolutely flooded with tourists doing the same Instagram shots as everyone else. The key is going to the quiet neighborhoods away from Old Town Square, drinking cheap Czech beer in basement pubs, and appreciating that your money goes three times further here than anywhere else in Europe.

Best For

  • Budget travelers (€3 beers, €5 dinners)
  • Architecture enthusiasts (Gothic, Baroque, weird art)
  • Nightlife explorers (underground clubs, beer culture)
  • Photography lovers (literally every street)

What Actually Made My Trip

Get Away from Old Town Square

It's packed and touristy. Cross the Charles Bridge and explore the neighborhoods on the other side. Smaller streets, real locals, weird cafés.

Astronomical Clock at Odd Hours

Go at 7 AM or 7 PM instead of midday when 10,000 people gather. It's actually cool when you can see it clearly.

Underground Brewery Taverns

Find a basement beer hall. €2–3 for amazing Czech beer. Sit for hours. Locals + tourists who found the right place.

Day Trip: Kutná Hora

Bone church and medieval mining town, 1 hour away by train. Wild, atmospheric, worth the half-day trip.

Where to Eat (& Drink)

Budget-Friendly (This is Where Prague Wins)

  • Goulash & bread - €4–6, warming and good
  • Trdelník - Sweet pastry, €2–3
  • Czech beer (Pilsner Urquell, Budvar) - €1–3 a pint
  • Cheap restaurant meals - €5–10 for full dinner

Worth Splurging On

  • Traditional Czech dinner - Still cheap, €8–15
  • Absinthe experience - €5–10 for tasting
  • Riverside restaurant - Splurge meal with views

Prague Specialty

  • Svíčková (sirloin in cream sauce)
  • Palačinka (Czech pancakes)
  • Tripe soup
  • Ice cream (incredibly good and cheap)

Where to Stay

Best Neighborhoods

Old Town (Staré Město): Touristy, expensive, but central. OK for a night or two.

Lesser Town (Malá Strana): Quieter, hilly, charming, good balance of accessibility + local feel.

Vinohrady: Hip neighborhood, good restaurants, actually where young Czechs live.

Avoid: New Town unless you want party hostels and drunk tourists.

Budget Options (So Cheap)

  • Hostels: €12–18/night (actually nice ones)
  • Airbnb: €20–40/night for rooms
  • Budget hotels: €30–50/night

Real Tips

  • Avoid tourist traps on Old Town Square. Restaurants with picture menus are always overpriced.
  • Czech is hard; English works in tourist areas. Try basic greetings, people appreciate it.
  • Buy a public transport pass. Trams and metro are efficient and cheap.
  • Beer is cheaper than water. Seriously. The water is fine but beer is €1–2.
  • Charles Bridge in early morning is beautiful. Go before 8 AM to see it relatively empty.
  • Prague Castle is worth a visit. Free to walk around the grounds, small fee to enter buildings.
  • Pickpockets target tourists on trams. Watch your stuff, especially tram 22.

Sample Weekend Plan

Friday Evening

Arrive, walk Charles Bridge at sunset, find an underground beer hall, cheap dinner and beers.

Saturday

Morning: Astronomical Clock (early or off-hours). Midday: Walk neighborhoods away from tourists. Afternoon: Prague Castle walk. Evening: Brewery tavern, more beer, find live music.

Sunday

Optional day trip: Kutná Hora (bone church + medieval town). Or sleep in, walk lesser-known neighborhoods, riverside walk. Evening dinner + easy night before leaving.
