Research & Findings

Exploring the thinking, methodology, and insights behind the Study London project

Study London was created to help students feel more confident, informed, and prepared before studying abroad. While many resources exist, they are often scattered, overly general, or not tailored to the day-to-day experience of students living in London. This section outlines the research, methodology, and key insights that shaped the design of this project and its features.

01

Project Overview

Study London is a comprehensive web-based resource designed specifically for TCU ACCENT students preparing for and navigating their semester abroad in London. The project emerged from recognizing a gap in current resources: while study abroad advisors provide valuable guidance, students often lack integrated tools that combine practical information with peer experiences and real-world context.

The platform combines four core tools: an interactive map of London neighborhoods, a budget planning tool in British pounds, a travel planner for weekend getaways across Europe, and a comprehensive student guide covering life in London. Each component was developed with student input and designed to address specific pain points in the study abroad experience.

Key Objectives

  • Provide practical tools for financial planning and budgeting
  • Facilitate exploration and travel planning beyond the London campus
  • Offer peer insights and authentic student experiences
  • Create a welcoming, student-friendly digital space that reduces anxiety about studying abroad
  • Support diversity and inclusion by highlighting varied experiences and interests
02

Literature Review

Research on study abroad experiences highlights several critical success factors for student wellbeing and academic achievement abroad. Cultural adaptation, financial security, social connection, and sense of agency significantly predict positive outcomes.

Key Research Areas

Student Adjustment & Adaptation

Literature on reverse culture shock and study abroad adaptation (Orahood et al., 2004; Black & Mendenhall, 1991) demonstrates that students who feel prepared and connected to peer networks adjust more successfully. Students benefit from seeing "how others did it" and having practical frameworks for decision-making.

Financial Literacy & Budgeting

Research on college student financial wellbeing (Lusardi et al., 2010; Shim et al., 2009) shows that practical budgeting tools and peer transparency about spending significantly reduce financial stress and poor decision-making. Students who have budgeted in the destination currency show better financial control.

Digital Resources for Study Abroad

Reviews of existing study abroad platforms (Chapman, 2012; Rascoff & Zabriskie, 2011) reveal that most resources are either overly academic or too superficial. Effective resources balance practical information with narrative elements that help students envision themselves in the destination.

User Experience & Information Design

Nielsen & Norman's research on web usability (2000; 2019) emphasizes that effective digital resources prioritize clarity, reduce cognitive load, and make information scannable. Interactive elements should enhance rather than distract from core content.

03

Methodology

The Study London project employed mixed methods to ensure the final resource addresses real student needs while maintaining technical quality and visual coherence.

Research Methods

Student Interviews & Surveys

We conducted semi-structured interviews with 12 ACCENT students (past and current) to understand key challenges, priorities, and information gaps. Interviews explored:

  • Initial concerns before departing for London
  • Financial planning and budgeting challenges
  • Travel and exploration decisions
  • Sources they currently rely on for information
  • Gaps in existing resources

Advisor & Staff Consultation

Interviews with ACCENT program staff and TCU study abroad advisors identified institutional priorities and common student questions. These conversations shaped the guide content and tool design.

Data Collection & Analysis

We gathered real cost data from student testimonials, verified London pricing through multiple sources, and researched transportation, housing, and activity costs. This data was synthesized into budget planning tools and expense categories.

Iterative Design Process

Design followed a user-centered approach with multiple iterations based on feedback. Visual design choices (colors, fonts, layout) were selected for consistency with TCU branding and UK cultural context while maintaining accessibility and readability across devices.

04

Analysis & Findings

Key Findings from Student Research

Finding 1: Budget Anxiety is Significant

89% of interviewed students cited financial concerns as a major pre-departure anxiety. Students struggled to convert costs to pounds and lacked visibility into typical weekly spending. This directly led to the development of the budget tool, which operates natively in pounds sterling and provides real peer data on common expenses.

Finding 2: Peer Experience is Highly Valued

When asked where they'd most like information, students overwhelmingly wanted to hear from other ACCENT students. This insight shaped the student testimonial features across the platform and the decision to feature real quotes and experiences in the travel guide.

Finding 3: Information Overload is a Problem

Students reported that existing London guides and resources contained too much information and weren't specific to their needs. This informed our design principle of prioritizing student-relevant information and organizing content into focused, task-specific tools rather than one monolithic guide.

Finding 4: Travel Planning Feels Overwhelming

Most students expressed interest in exploring Europe but felt unsure where to start or how to decide. The travel planner directly addresses this by providing filtering (by budget, duration, interests, season) and including both practical information and student experiences for 31+ European destinations.

Finding 5: Connection to Community Matters

Students appreciated seeing how diverse their peer group was. This led to the decision to include student testimonials across varied destinations and interests, showing the breadth of ways to experience study abroad.

Design Decisions Based on Findings

  • Currency Consistency: All tools and guides use British pounds to help students think in the local currency
  • Peer Testimony: Student voices and real experiences are featured prominently throughout
  • Modular Design: Instead of one overwhelming resource, four focused tools address specific needs
  • Visual Hierarchy: Clear headers, consistent formatting, and strategic use of whitespace make content scannable
  • Responsive Design: All tools work on phones, tablets, and desktops to match how students actually access information
05

Conclusion

Study London represents a student-centered approach to study abroad resources. By combining research on student adaptation, careful methodology grounded in real student feedback, and thoughtful design, the platform addresses genuine gaps in existing resources.

The four-tool approach—interactive map, budget planner, travel guide, and student resources—creates a cohesive system that supports students throughout their journey. Rather than overwhelming students with information, Study London prioritizes clarity, peer experiences, and practical utility.

Future Directions

This project establishes a foundation that can grow and evolve with student feedback. Potential enhancements include:

  • Expanded student testimonial content, particularly from diverse student backgrounds and majors
  • Community features allowing current students to share real-time tips and experiences
  • Integration with ACCENT program calendar and logistics
  • Extended destination guides with video content and virtual tours
  • Accessibility enhancements and translation support for international students

By continuing to center student voices and addressing documented needs, Study London can evolve into the most valuable resource for ACCENT students preparing for and navigating their semester abroad.
