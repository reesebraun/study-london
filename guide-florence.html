Florence

The Art History Overload

Best for: Art lovers, Renaissance nerds, wine enthusiasts • 2–3 days

The Vibe

Florence is the birthplace of the Renaissance, and honestly, it feels like it. Every corner has a sculpture, every museum has a masterpiece, and you'll hit "art overload" around day two. It's also incredibly touristy—streets packed with tour groups, overpriced restaurants in the center, shops selling the same Duomo trinkets.

But here's the thing: if you skip the obvious stuff, find the small museums and neighborhoods outside the center, and actually sit and absorb instead of rushing, Florence is genuinely magic. It's about appreciating art and history, not checking boxes.

Best For

  • Art enthusiasts (Renaissance, Uffizi Gallery, Accademia)
  • History buffs (Medici family, Renaissance history)
  • Wine lovers (Tuscany nearby, Chianti region)
  • Slow travelers (absorb, don't rush)

What Actually Made My Trip

Uffizi Gallery (Book Ahead, Go Early)

It's packed but genuinely one of the best art museums in the world. Botticelli, da Vinci, Raphael—all here. Book online, go at opening time (8 AM), spend 2–3 hours, then leave before the crowds get chaotic.

Skip the Duomo, Climb Piazzale Michelangelo

The Duomo is pretty from outside but tourist hell inside. Instead, walk up to Piazzale Michelangelo (on the south side) for the best view of the whole city. Free, amazing, way fewer people.

Oltrarno Neighborhood

Cross the Arno to the quieter side. Artisan shops, smaller churches, local vibe. Ponte Vecchio has jewelry shops, but the real Florence is on the other side.

Day Trip: Tuscany Countryside

Train to Chianti region for wine tasting and rolling hills. Greve in Chianti is a good hub. Sunflower fields in summer are stunning.

Where to Eat (& Drink)

Budget-Friendly

  • Ribollita - Tuscan vegetable soup, €5–8
  • Panini - Great sandwiches, €4–7
  • Osteria food - Simple, good, cheap, €8–15
  • Chianti wine - €4–8 a glass, or €8–15 a bottle

Worth Splurging On

  • Proper Tuscan dinner - Bistecca alla fiorentina (steak), €20–35
  • Wine tasting in Chianti - €10–25 depending on vineyard
  • Gelato from quality place - €3–4 but worth it

Tuscan Specialties

  • Bistecca alla fiorentina (big grilled steak)
  • Pappardelle (wide pasta with wild boar sauce)
  • Trippa alla fiorentina (tripe)
  • Pecorino cheese

Where to Stay

Best Neighborhoods

Centro (Center): Tourist hub, Duomo area, expensive, convenient but chaotic.

Oltrarno: South of the Arno, quieter, more local, better restaurants.

San Frediano: Young neighborhood, good bars and restaurants, walk-able to center.

San Marco: North of center, quieter, still accessible.

Budget Options

  • Hostels: €18–25/night
  • Airbnb: €35–60/night
  • Budget hotels: €50–80/night

Real Tips

  • Book museum tickets online. Lines are long, online booking saves hours.
  • Skip restaurant menus with pictures near tourist sites. Find small osterie away from Duomo.
  • Florence Uffizi Gallery early morning is essential. 8 AM opening, go immediately.
  • Gelato is everywhere but quality varies. Natural colors (not bright) = better gelato.
  • Ponte Vecchio at night is beautiful. Walk it around dusk for fewer crowds and amazing light.
  • Buses are confusing. Walking is actually the best way to see Florence.
  • Chianti wine region is doable on a train. Worth a day trip for wine + countryside.

Sample Weekend Plan

Friday Evening

Arrive, walk around, cross Ponte Vecchio at sunset, dinner in Oltrarno neighborhood, explore local bars.

Saturday

Morning: Uffizi Gallery at 8 AM opening (book ahead!). Afternoon: Walk around, explore neighborhoods away from tourists. Evening: Piazzale Michelangelo for sunset view, dinner, early night to recover from art overload.

Sunday

Optional day trip to Chianti (train to Greve, wine tasting, countryside). Or sleep in, visit smaller museums (Bargello, Palazzo Pitti), leisurely afternoon. Evening: Final dinner, pack for travel.
