Student Travel Guide

Real insights from study abroad students about seven European cities.

The Study London Guide

If you're planning trips while studying abroad, this is the kind of advice I wish I had before going. Every city feels completely different, and the best trips happen when you balance the big "must-sees" with time to just explore.

London Paris Dublin Prague Budapest Lisbon Florence

🇬🇧 London — Your Home Base

London honestly becomes your comfort city. At first it feels big and a little overwhelming, but once you get used to it, it's one of the easiest places to live and explore.

What makes London different is that every area feels like a different city. One day you're doing tourist stuff around Westminster, the next you're in Notting Hill or Shoreditch and it feels completely different.

You're not going to see everything — and that's fine. The best way to do London is mixing a few major landmarks with slower days walking through neighborhoods, markets, and parks.

What actually made my trip:

  • Walking through Hyde Park after traveling
  • Borough Market (go hungry, trust)
  • Notting Hill + Portobello Road
  • Ending nights with a good dinner + views (Shard / river walk)

Real tips:

  • The Tube is your best friend
  • Distances are way bigger than you think
  • Always have a backup plan for weather
  • You'll end up at pubs more than clubs (and it's better)

🇫🇷 Paris — Slower, Romantic, Worth Wandering

Paris is less about checking things off and more about just being there. Some of the best moments aren't planned — they're just walking, sitting at a café, or finding a random street that feels like a movie.

That said, you do need to plan a little. Paris fills up fast, and not booking things ahead can mean missing out.

What actually made my trip:

  • Versailles (wish I had more time there)
  • Café hopping and slow mornings
  • Walking along the Seine at sunset

Real tips:

  • Book big things like the Louvre and Dior early
  • Stay in a local area, not just tourist zones
  • Expect things to be spread out
  • Go in spring if you can — rain changes everything

🇮🇪 Dublin — Fun, Social, Not That Deep

Dublin is one of those places where you don't go for a packed itinerary — you go for the experience. It's smaller, super social, and honestly all about the pub culture.

It's not the kind of city where you're running around seeing everything. It's more about being with people, hopping between places, and enjoying the vibe.

What actually made my trip:

  • Guinness Storehouse (way better than expected)
  • Temple Bar area at night
  • Just walking into random pubs

Real tips:

  • Don't overplan this one
  • Best as a quick weekend trip
  • Expect lots of American students
  • The energy is what makes it worth it

🇨🇿 Prague — Straight Out of a Fairytale

Prague is one of those cities where you're just constantly like… wait this is real??

It's super walkable, not overly complicated, and honestly one of the easiest places to explore without a plan.

What actually made my trip:

  • Walking everywhere (no Uber needed)
  • Prague Castle at sunset (best view)
  • Pub crawl — so fun and super social

Real tips:

  • You can do most of it in 1–2 days
  • Go early to avoid crowds
  • It's cheaper than most cities
  • Nights are just as fun as daytime

🇭🇺 Budapest — Underrated + Best Night Views

Budapest surprised me. It's not packed with things to do, but everything feels really dramatic and pretty — especially at night.

The river splits the city, and walking along it or seeing it from above is honestly what makes the trip.

What actually made my trip:

  • Fisherman's Bastion views
  • Champagne cruise at night (MUST)
  • Walking everywhere instead of Uber

Real tips:

  • 1–2 days is enough
  • Skip overhyped restaurants
  • Focus on views + experiences
  • Nights here are unreal

🇵🇹 Lisbon — Chill, Sunny, and Easy

Lisbon feels like a break from the faster cities. It's slower, warmer, and more about just enjoying where you are.

There's not a crazy amount to do, which is honestly what makes it nice.

What actually made my trip:

  • Sintra day trip (Regaleira was unreal)
  • Sunset boat cruise
  • Rooftop bars at night

Real tips:

  • Hills are intense — wear good shoes
  • Ubers are super cheap
  • Perfect place to slow down
  • Food + wine is some of the best

🇮🇹 Florence — Food, Wine, and Vibes

Florence feels smaller and more personal than other cities. It's less about running around and more about enjoying food, drinks, and the atmosphere.

It does feel very study abroad-heavy, but honestly that makes it fun.

What actually made my trip:

  • Piazzale Michelangelo at sunset
  • Wine tour in Tuscany
  • Pasta making class

Real tips:

  • Don't overpack your schedule
  • Shopping is unreal (leather!!)
  • Food is the highlight
  • Perfect for a fun, slower weekend

✈️ Final Advice

The biggest mistake you can make is over-planning everything. You need a few anchor plans — reservations and key sights — but the best parts of every trip are usually the ones you didn't expect.

Every city feels different, and that's what makes traveling during study abroad so fun. Some places are fast-paced, some are slower, but they all add something completely different to your experience.
