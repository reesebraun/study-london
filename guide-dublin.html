Dublin

Good Craic City

Best for: Nightlife, history, Irish culture, pub lovers • 2–3 days

The Vibe

Dublin is chaos in the best way. It's smaller and scrappier than London or Paris, but somehow way more fun. Everyone goes to the Temple Bar area first—don't stay there. Real Dublin is in the neighborhoods, the pubs that have been there for 200 years, and the weird mix of Georgian architecture and modern bars.

The thing about Dublin is that everyone's friendly and will talk to you. The pub culture is actual culture, not just drinking. You'll end up having conversations with locals about Irish history and politics whether you plan to or not.

Best For

  • Nightlife enthusiasts (best pub scene in Europe, honestly)
  • History buffs (Georgian architecture, literary history)
  • Social travelers (locals are incredibly friendly)
  • Short trips (compact, walkable, 2–3 days is plenty)

What Actually Made My Trip

Pub Crawl (But Actually Good Ones)

Skip the organized tours. Just walk down the Liffey or through Temple Bar hitting random pubs. You'll find better spots and actual locals.

Guinness Storehouse

Touristy but actually worth it for the 360° view of Dublin from the top. The pint at the top tastes better (maybe psychologically).

Book of Kells at Trinity

Early morning visit to beat crowds. The manuscripts are genuinely stunning. Quick visit, worth it.

Walk to Howth

DART train out to the coast. Cliffs, walks, fresh air, fish and chips by the water. Half-day trip, totally worth it.

Where to Eat (& Drink)

Budget-Friendly

  • Irish stew & soda bread - €8–12 at any pub
  • Fish & chips - €6–10, best by the water in Howth
  • Pint of Guinness - €5–7 (pricier tourist areas, cheaper in real pubs)
  • Coddle - Dublin specialty, cheap comfort food, €6–9

Worth Splurging On

  • Seafood restaurant - Dublin has incredible fish (€20–35)
  • Traditional Irish dinner - Boxty, colcannon, proper fare
  • Live music venue with dinner - Temple Bar or side streets (€25–40)

Pub Etiquette

  • Go to the bar, order yourself (no table service usually)
  • Chat with whoever's sitting next to you
  • Tip isn't mandatory but €1–2 is nice
  • Try: Smithwick's, local cider, Irish whiskey

Where to Stay

Best Neighborhoods

Temple Bar area: Touristy, overpriced, but central. Skip it.

South of the Liffey: Grogan's area, more local, cheaper, better.

Northside: Smithfield, O'Connell Street area—up and coming, quieter.

South Side: Around Trinity, walkable, lots of students.

Budget Options

  • Hostels: €18–25/night (Generator is solid again)
  • Airbnb: €35–60/night
  • Budget hotels: €50–75/night

Real Tips

  • Irish people are genuinely nice. Say hello, chat, be open to conversation.
  • The DART is great for day trips. Howth, Dun Laoghaire, Greystones—all accessible.
  • Avoid Temple Bar for real experience. It's chain bars and tourists. Go to actual neighborhood pubs.
  • Sunday roasts are a thing. Many pubs do amazing Sunday dinners for €12–18.
  • Georgian architecture is everywhere. Just walking is a mini history lesson.
  • Buses are confusing but cheap. Get a Leap card, or just ask locals for directions.
  • Rain is consistent. Bring a jacket, it's part of the Dublin experience.

Sample Weekend Plan

Friday Evening

Arrive, walk along the Liffey, settle in, grab dinner and a pint at a neighborhood pub (not Temple Bar).

Saturday

Morning: Trinity & Book of Kells. Afternoon: Guinness Storehouse + view. Evening: Pub crawl through real Dublin neighborhoods, live music if you find it.

Sunday

Morning: Sleep in, late breakfast. Day trip: DART to Howth (cliffs, walks, fish & chips). Evening: Easy pub dinner, pack for travel.
